Number Types and Operations

1. Understanding Different Number Types

  • Natural Numbers (N): Counting numbers (1, 2, 3, ...).
  • Whole Numbers (W): Natural numbers including zero (0, 1, 2, ...).
  • Integers (Z): Positive and negative whole numbers (..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...).
  • Rational Numbers (Q): Numbers that can be written as fractions (¬Ω, 0.75, -3, 5/8).
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be written as fractions (e.g., œÄ, ‚àö2).
  • Real Numbers (R): All rational and irrational numbers.

2. Factors, Multiples, and Prime Numbers

  • Factors: Numbers that divide exactly into another number (e.g., Factors of 12 ‚Üí 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12).
  • Multiples: Numbers found in the multiplication table of a given number (e.g., Multiples of 4 ‚Üí 4, 8, 12, 16, ...).
  • Prime Numbers: Numbers with only two factors (1 and itself), e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, ...
  • HCF (Highest Common Factor): Largest number that divides two or more numbers.
  • LCM (Lowest Common Multiple): Smallest common multiple in both numbers.
